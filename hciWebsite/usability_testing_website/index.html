Dylan Tomlinson - Usability Testing Website

Welcome to my website dedicated to reviewing and analyzing the Snap On website. My goal is to examine the design, usability, and functionality of Snap On's website. As a well-known and highly regarded brand, Snap On tools demand a premium price point. However, despite its reputation, the website design falls short in various aspects.

Visit Snap On

Why Snap On?

I have chosen to focus on the Snap On website due to its status as a large brand that surprisingly showcases a poor website design. I aim to conduct a heuristic evaluation and provide constructive feedback to help identify areas of improvement.

Strengths and Weaknesses

While the Snap On website has several weaknesses, it does have a few areas where it performs relatively well. One well executed aspect is its provision of necessary information on franchising opportunities. Additionally, the product page is presented in an organized and visually appealing manner, allowing users to browse through their offerings.

However, the website falls short in conveying its purpose clearly to users. The overall design lacks flexibility, making it challenging for users to navigate and find what they are looking for. The design choices are cluttered and do not prioritize clean and functional layouts. Furthermore, the website's overall functionality remains unclear, leading to confusion and frustration for users.

Usability Testing

I will also be constructing usability tests to further analyze the user experience of the Snap On website. By conducting these tests, I aim to identify specific pain points and gather valuable insights to offer recommendations for enhancing usability and user satisfaction.

Please explore this website to observe our findings and make your own conclusion about the effectiveness of Snap On's website.
